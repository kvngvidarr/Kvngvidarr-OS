KEY CORRECTIONS TO COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS
Rate Hike ‚âÝ Bullish Market. Rate hike = USD‚Üë (bullish currency) BUT Stocks‚Üì (bearish equities) AND Gold‚Üì (bearish commodities). Never conflate currency bullish with market bullish.
CPI reaction = Surprise, not level. If CPI expected at 3.2% and prints 3.8% ‚Üí USD‚Üë‚Üë. If CPI expected 3.8% and prints 3.8% ‚Üí muted. Market already priced it. The surprise delta is what moves price.
Oil in Risk-Off: Oil‚Üë during geopolitical crisis ‚Üí Gold‚Üë (fear trade). Oil‚Üë from inflation ‚Üí Gold‚Üì (yields rise). Same oil move, opposite gold reaction. The cause is the variable, not the price.
Stagflation trap: High inflation + weak growth = Fed in a bind. Hiking too hard ‚Üí recession. Pausing ‚Üí inflation stays. No clean trade. Reduce size.

REAL YIELD (KEY CONCEPT)
Real Yield = Nominal Yield ‚àí Inflation. Gold is most sensitive to REAL yields, not nominal. When real yields rise (yields up faster than inflation) ‚Üí Gold falls hard. When real yields fall (inflation rising faster than yields) ‚Üí Gold rallies regardless of nominal yield direction.
